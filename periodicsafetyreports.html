About Periodic Safety Reports

Periodic Safety Reports are essential regulatory documents that provide a comprehensive and critical evaluation of the benefit-risk balance of medicinal products. These reports are required by global health authorities to ensure ongoing monitoring of product safety throughout its lifecycle. At Sojar Research, we offer end-to-end support for the preparation, review, and submission of all major periodic safety reports, ensuring compliance with the latest international guidelines.

Our Periodic Safety Report Services

PSUR / PBRER

Preparation of Periodic Safety Update Reports (PSUR) and Periodic Benefit-Risk Evaluation Reports (PBRER) in accordance with ICH E2C(R2) guidelines, including data analysis, signal detection, and benefit-risk evaluation.

DSUR

Development Safety Update Reports (DSUR) for investigational products, ensuring compliance with ICH E2F and local regulatory requirements, with expert data integration and medical writing.

Risk Management Plan (RMP)

Comprehensive Risk Management Plans (RMP) including safety specification, pharmacovigilance plan, and risk minimization measures, tailored to product and market needs.

Signal Detection & Management

Advanced signal detection, evaluation, and management services to identify and assess new safety information, supporting proactive risk mitigation.

Medical Writing & Literature Review

Expert medical writing for all periodic safety reports, including literature review, data interpretation, and preparation of submission-ready documents.

Regulatory Submission Support

End-to-end support for electronic and paper submissions to global health authorities, ensuring timely and compliant delivery of all safety reports.
